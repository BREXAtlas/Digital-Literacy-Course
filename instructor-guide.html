Instructor Guide

This page summarizes the instructor guide. The full version, including facilitation questions and the curriculum map, lives in docs/INSTRUCTOR_PILOT_GUIDE.md and docs/CURRICULUM_MAP.md.

Purpose and audience

Ram Ready Digital Literacy is an independent, open-source pilot built with first-year Angelo State students in mind, while remaining openly accessible to the public. It teaches file, communication, research, privacy, security, and AI habits college and work expect.

Suggested uses

  • First-year seminar supplement
  • New-student orientation module
  • Library instruction session
  • Writing-center or academic-advising resource
  • IT/cybersecurity awareness training
  • Faculty AI-policy preparation discussion

Small library pilot

A first pilot can assign different students and reviewers to different lesson ranges rather than asking everyone to test only the landing page. The project includes a detailed testing checklist and pilot feedback protocol.

The structured Pilot Feedback Form measures organization, pacing, assessment alignment, usefulness, relevance, participation, visuals, accessibility, source trust, technical reliability, perceived learning change, bugs, and requested features. The form prepares an email to lmcgaffie@angelo.edu and does not store the response on the site.

Course-specific AI rules

This course does not define any instructor's official AI-use policy. Course-specific AI rules may differ by instructor and assignment — the current syllabus, assignment directions, instructor guidance, and official university policies always control.

Facilitating digital access inequity

When discussing file storage, devices, or software access, avoid assuming every student has unlimited storage, a personal laptop, high-speed internet, or paid software. The course explicitly avoids platform marketing and does not shame learners with limited devices, bandwidth, or technical experience.

Reporting a content error

Use the pilot feedback form for student and library testing. Maintainers and technical reviewers may also use the GitHub issue templates in this repository: Curriculum Review, Source Correction, or Accessibility Review. See the source review checklist.

Limits of the simulation

All simulation variables are illustrative teaching simplifications, not a formal evaluation of a learner's intelligence, employability, or academic standing. No path in the course promotes itself as the universally correct one, and no setback in the story is an irreversible dead end.
